The engineering behind our sustainable Trope.

⚙️ Torque

These documents detail the mechanics of a turbine requiring a torque of 0.95 N·m. By applying a radius of 0.2 m at a 90° angle, the necessary rotational force is calculated as 4.75 N. Additionally, using the pressure difference and radius, the required surface area is determined to be 0.316 m².

🌬️ Venturi effect

These documents explain how the Venturi effect and Bernoulli’s principle drive the turbine. As air moves from a narrow inlet to a wider outlet, its velocity changes from 4.72 m/s to 5.8 m/s, creating a pressure drop from 101,341 Pa to 101,319.5 Pa. This resulting pressure gradient generates the force necessary to rotate the turbine around its fixed axis

🌀 Angular momentum

This document explains how angular momentum ($40.4\text{ kg}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{s}$) provides stability for turbines in turbulent urban areas like Helwan. By maximizing the moment of inertia through a hollow spherical design, the system maintains consistent rotation and electrical output despite fluctuating wind speeds and directions.

⚡ Power collection

The document discusses Power Collection, utilizing the Betz Power Equation to determine theoretical wind energy. While the prototype generated a theoretical 0.98W, the actual electrical output was 0.5W. This results in a 51% power coefficient (C p), reflecting high efficiency in converting kinetic energy into usable electricity.
